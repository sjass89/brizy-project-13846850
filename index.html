ORGANIZATIONS

Doro Foundation is a national nonprofit that helps philanthropic individuals donate life insurance as a major gift (even term policies) that provides immediate funds and requires no further premium payments.

WHAT POLICIES QUALIFY

Both term & permanent policies can qualify for this option. Even though term policies do not have any cash value, there may be significant appreciated value to be found through our major gift program.

WHO QUALIFIES

Typically, the insured on the policy must be over the age of 65 and have some health conditions that began after the policy was originally issued. While not everyone qualifies, our process will find out.

WHAT IS THE BENEFIT

Life insurance policies that qualify receive on average 8X the cash surrender value or 20% of the policy face amount. For example, a $1,000,000 term life policy could hold $200,000 of value. The big factor is health and the Fair Market Value (FMV) can be significantly higher or lower.

WHAT IS FAIR MARKET VALUE

Fair Market Value (FMV) is how much the open market would offer for an asset. In the case of life insurance there are many mutual funds, pension plans, banks and other investors looking to purchase life insurance policies as an investment. The highest FMV is determined through a competitive process.

WHO WE ARE

The Doro Foundation is a national nonprofit that was created to manage the process of receiving, servicing, liquidating and lastly granting funds from life insurance donations.

WHAT WE DO

We enable other nonprofits to outsource the expertise needed and potential risks of accepting donations of life insurance. Our team is highly experienced in this process and ensures the best experience for all.

WHY WE DO IT

Each year policies that could be sold for over $30 billion ($180 billion of face amount) are lapsed. This is an enormous philanthropic opportunity we support and are passionate about doing the right way.
